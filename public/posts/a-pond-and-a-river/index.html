    A pond and a river

    A pond and a river

    2020-10-14
    Life
    literature, poem

    I pondered a lot about the pale thin limbs of hers, of curves that blends into mine.
    I coil around her eyes possessed of a deepest black,
    soaked with the obsessive fixation on outcry.
    Yet her blood from screaming is asphalt grey.
    It’s greying, it’s the grass by the pond.
    I am in love with her sudden burst of emotion of unknown origin.
    Deep in the furs,
    a pair of blood-filled eyes in pride and intransigence,
    still more,
    with sorrow that arise at the sight of unnamed rivers, topped with cremated foam and mossy algae.
    Singsongly she said:
    A Buddha was buried here, together with a secret pact with the wind.
